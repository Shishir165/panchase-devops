About Panchase

A biodiversity hotspot in Nepal's heart

Panchase is a remarkable ecological region spreading across Kaski, Syangja and Parbat districts in the foothills of Pokhara, Nepal. Home to rare Himalayan flora and fauna, ancient forests and traditional farming communities.

We hosted Nepal's first biodiversity conference at the foothills of Panchase — bringing together conservationists, researchers, farmers and tourism experts to shape the future of this extraordinary landscape.

"To protect Panchase is to protect the living memory of Nepal's natural heritage."

Our organic farm operates without chemicals or pesticides — practicing traditional Nepali farming that has nourished communities here for generations, now shared with conscious travellers from around the world.
